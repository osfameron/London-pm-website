The Different Kinds of Meetings

London.pm organises several kind of in-person meetups:

  • Social Meetings once a month
  • Technical Meetings every two months
  • Ad-hoc Meetings typically when out-of-town visting Perl Mongers are in in town
  • Conferences such as the London Perl Workshop

    • Forthcoming Meetings

    Forthcoming meetings are announced on the announce mailing list

    Social Meetings

    Social meetings happen every month on the day after the first Wednesday of the month (i.e. the first Thursday of the month that doesn't land on the very first day of the month.)

    Social meet are a chance for the various members of the group to meet up face to face and chat with each other about things - both Perl and (mostly) non-Perl - and newcomers are more than welcome. The monthly meets tend to be bigger than the other ad hoc meetings that take place at other times, and we make sure that they're in easy to get to locations and the pub serves food (meaning that people can eat in the bar if they want to). They normally start around 6.30pm (or whenever people get there after work) and a group tends to be left come closing time.

    If you're a newcomer or other first timer (even if you've been lurking on the mailing list or on IRC) then please seek our Glorious Leader Tom out - we have a tradition that the leader of this motley crew buys the new people a drink and introduces them to people.

    Technical Meetings

    Technical meetings happen every two months. They take place in the evening, typically starting around 6:30pm - 7pm and lasting little over a couple of hours.

    The technical meetings are a chance to find out what has been going on in the Perl community, what techniques people are using and how Perl integrates with other software. They consist of numerous technical presentations given by members of the group or by guest speakers on Perl or topics that might interest Perl programmers. In some respects they can be thought of as a very small conference.

    Techmeets usually occur in larger office spaces graciously donated for the evening by the employers of Perl friendly companies. As such we often have to ask attendees to sign up via a free web form in advanced to comply with the security policies of the venues.

    First time speakers are highly encouraged at techmeets (even if all you want to give is a five minute 'lightning' talk.) A call for participation is posted to the main list some time prior to the tech meet itself.

    Ad-Hoc Meetings

    Ad-Hoc meetings are normally organised when a Perl Monger from out of town is in London on a day other than the social meetings. They also occur around other events which many London.pm members may frequent (i.e. people may meet up at Linux conferences or even to go see the latest geek movie..)

    If you are visiting London and cannot join us for a social meeting, consider posting to the main list so a ad-hoc meeting can be arranged.

    One regular ad-hoc meeting (if you'll excuse the tautology) is the Heretics pub meet which occurs on any Thursday 1st.

    Open for all

    No matter which meeting you choose, there's no requirement that you are proficient with Perl in order to turn up (in fact, several of our regular attendees haven't been technical at all, they've just enjoyed our company.) We're a welcoming lot (we even have a friendly code of conduct for all our meetings.) In short: we look forward to seeing you.
